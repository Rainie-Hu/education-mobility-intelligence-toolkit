## Recommendation Letter Strategy

### Target: 5-6 Letters

#### Letter 1-2: Independent Academic Experts (Time-Series / Statistics)

**Who to target:**
- Professors in statistics, econometrics, or time-series analysis
- Researchers who presented at ISF or whose work the toolkit cites
- Do NOT need to know you personally — "independent expert review" letters are standard
- Ivy League is a plus but not required; any R1 university professor in the field works

**What they should address:**
- StationarityToolkit fills a methodological gap (consolidating 10 tests into one framework)
- The ISF presentation demonstrates academic engagement
- The work advances time-series methodology applicable to forecasting in critical domains
- Practical value for researchers and practitioners

**How to find them:**
- ISF co-presenters or attendees
- Authors of papers your toolkit cites or builds upon
- Attorney networks (many NIW attorneys have academic contacts)

---

#### Letter 3: Industry Expert in Credential Evaluation / Education Data

**Who to target:**
- Senior leader at another credential evaluation organization (not WES)
- University admissions leader who uses credential evaluations
- Leader at NACES, AICE, or similar professional bodies
- Government agency contact in immigration or education policy

**What they should address:**
- Credential evaluation is critical national infrastructure
- Data-driven modernization of this space is urgently needed
- The field lacks people combining data science expertise with credential evaluation domain knowledge
- Your contributions represent meaningful advancement

---

#### Letter 4: WES Leadership (Dependent)

**Who to target:**
- VP or Director level — senior enough to be credible, close enough to know your work

**What they should address:**
- Specific impact of your projects with real numbers
- How your work differs from typical analyst output (innovation, not just execution)
- Scale of operations supported (400K+ applicants annually)
- Your unique contributions to WES's data infrastructure and forecasting capabilities

---

#### Letter 5: Former Employer (Circle K or Redwood)

**Who to target:**
- Manager or Director from Circle K (3-year tenure is substantial)

**What they should address:**
- Track record of building impactful data systems predates WES
- Specific projects/models built and their business impact
- Demonstrates a sustained pattern of contribution

---

#### Letter 6: Economic Impact / Policy Expert

**Who to target:**
- Economist or policy researcher studying immigration, workforce integration, or international education
- Think tanks: Brookings, Migration Policy Institute, NFAP
- University-based immigration/labor economists

**What they should address:**
- Economic value of efficient credential evaluation to the U.S. economy
- How delays/inaccuracies in credential evaluation harm workforce integration
- Data-driven improvements in this space have measurable national economic benefit
- Quantify the problem where possible (e.g., underemployment costs of skilled immigrants)

**Note:** This may be commissioned as a formal economic impact declaration. Typical cost: $1,500-3,000. Discuss with attorney.

## Key Reminders

- **Letters > prestige.** A specific, detailed letter from an engaged professor at a good state school outweighs a generic paragraph from a famous name.
- **Independence matters.** Letters from people with no professional relationship to you carry more weight with adjudicators.
- **Draft templates for recommenders.** Most will appreciate having a starting point. Ensure each letter is specific to your work, not generic praise.
- **The blog post (Option A) must link your toolkit to your endeavor.** Frame it as: "Here's how stationarity testing improves forecasting in credential evaluation" — not just a toolkit tutorial.
- **JOSS acceptance is the #1 priority.** Everything else is secondary to getting that paper published.
