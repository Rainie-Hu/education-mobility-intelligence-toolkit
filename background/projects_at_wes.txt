Project c
International Shipment Pricing and Volume Forecasting Model for Credential Evaluation Delivery Operations
Business Problem
World Education Services needed to optimize the international distribution process for physical credential evaluation reports while reducing operational costs and maintaining delivery reliability for applicants and institutional recipients worldwide. The organization was evaluating a transition from an existing Canada-based shipment vendor to a lower-cost India-based logistics provider for shipments across Asia, including East Asia, South Asia, and Southeast Asia.
Accurate shipment forecasting and pricing analysis were critical because vendor contract terms depended on projected shipment volumes and pricing commitments.
My Role
Led the analytical validation and refinement of the shipment pricing and forecasting model used to evaluate the proposed logistics partnership. Conducted detailed review of shipment volume assumptions, pricing structures, and projected operational costs to support executive decision-making and vendor contract negotiations.
Methods / Tools Used
SQL, Snowflake, pricing analytics, shipment volume forecasting, financial modeling, operational data validation, business logic auditing, scenario analysis, cost optimization analysis.
Outcome Metrics
•	Identified critical inaccuracies in an existing shipment volume model developed by another team 
•	Corrected projected shipment volume estimates that significantly affected vendor contract assumptions 
•	Improved reliability of shipment forecasting and pricing calculations used in logistics planning 
•	Supported estimation of operational cost savings and shipment allocation across multiple Asian regions 
Business Impact
Helped prevent substantial operational and financial risk associated with inaccurate shipment volume commitments in third-party vendor negotiations. The corrected model reduced the likelihood of contract misalignment, financial overcommitment, and reputational risk with external logistics partners.
The project also supported more cost-efficient international delivery operations for credential evaluation reports used by international students, immigrants, universities, employers, and licensing institutions. By improving shipment pricing intelligence and operational forecasting, the work contributed to more scalable and sustainable delivery infrastructure supporting global credential evaluation services.
Who Used It
Director-level leadership, operations teams, finance stakeholders, logistics planning teams, vendor management stakeholders, and external shipment partners involved in international credential evaluation delivery operations.
Project D
Digital Delivery Financial Strategy and Pricing Model
Business Problem
World Education Services sought to modernize the delivery of credential evaluation reports by transitioning from paper-based international mail distribution to a digital delivery infrastructure. The initiative aimed to improve applicant experience, reduce international shipping delays, lower operational costs, and increase delivery reliability through a secure digital credential access system.
However, physical shipment and handling fees represented a significant revenue stream for the organization. Transitioning to digital delivery required the development of a comprehensive pricing and financial strategy to ensure the sustainability of the new delivery model while maintaining overall business performance.
My Role
Led the development of a large-scale financial and pricing model used to evaluate the feasibility, operational impact, and revenue implications of transitioning from physical to digital credential delivery systems. Worked cross-functionally with leadership, finance, product, operations, and IT stakeholders to assess pricing structures and business-wide operational dependencies.
Methods / Tools Used
Excel financial modeling, SQL, Python, Snowflake, pricing analytics, scenario modeling, operational cost analysis, revenue impact analysis, business process mapping, strategic forecasting.
Outcome Metrics
•  Developed enterprise-level financial and pricing models supporting digital delivery transformation initiatives affecting approximately 400,000 credential evaluation applicants annually 
•  Evaluated operational and revenue impacts across a global shipment and handling ecosystem representing tens of millions of dollars in annual service-related costs and fees 
•  Modeled pricing strategies designed to offset potential revenue displacement associated with reduced physical shipment and handling dependency 
•  Supported strategic analysis of organization-wide delivery workflows spanning evaluation reports, extra copy orders, upgrades, and international shipment operations 
•  Projected significant reductions in international credential delivery wait times by transitioning from cross-border physical mail fulfillment to near-instant digital access 
•  Evaluated opportunities to improve accessibility and delivery reliability for applicants in regions with unstable or delayed international shipping infrastructure 
•  Supported modernization efforts aimed at improving timely access to credential evaluation reports used in education admissions, professional licensing, employment verification, and immigration-related processes 
•  Produced strategic forecasting and financial feasibility analyses used by executive leadership to evaluate large-scale digital credential delivery infrastructure initiativesBusiness Impact
Enabled data-driven strategic planning for the modernization of international credential delivery infrastructure. The project supported evaluation of a scalable digital delivery system designed to improve service accessibility for international students, immigrants, universities, employers, and licensing institutions relying on credential evaluation reports.
The work also helped leadership assess the balance between operational efficiency gains and revenue sustainability during a large-scale transition from physical to digital service delivery. By modeling organization-wide financial implications, the project contributed to long-term planning around digital credential infrastructure and modernization of global evaluation services.
Who Used It
Executive leadership, finance teams, product teams, operations stakeholders, IT project teams, and strategic planning stakeholders involved in enterprise digital transformation initiatives.
